# Amazon-food-review-sentiment-analysis--Text-mining-and-predictive-modelling
Amazon Food Reviews Sentiment analysis: Text Mining & Business IntelligenceThis project leverages a dataset of over 568,000 Amazon product reviews to identify the linguistic drivers of customer satisfaction. By applying text mining and predictive modeling, the project transforms raw consumer sentiment into actionable intelligence for product development and quality assurance.## Business ImpactThis analysis provides a framework for manufacturers to move beyond simple star ratings and understand the why behind customer behavior:Quality Optimization: Identifies specific ingredient-related drivers—such as "corn," "meal," and "meat"—that directly correlate with sentiment in high-performing categories like Dog Food.Customer Experience (CX) Strategy: Distinguishes between linguistic patterns of "satisfaction" and "disappointment" to guide targeted product improvements.Operational Efficiency: Demonstrates that specific text-weighting techniques (like Entropy) can improve model accuracy for certain products, allowing for more reliable automated feedback loops.## Technical MethodologyThe project follows a rigorous data science pipeline to ensure model interpretability and performance.### 1. Data Engineering & Pre-processingTarget Engineering: Created a binary sentiment target where scores $\ge 4$ are Positive (1) and $< 4$ are Negative (0).Linguistic Parsing: Implemented tokenization, lemmatization, and custom filtering to remove noise (e.g., HTML tags and generic terms like "product").Partitioning: Utilized a 70/30 stratified train/test split to maintain class balance across categories.### 2. Feature ExtractionLatent Semantic Analysis: Transformed text into 50 Singular Value Decompositions (SVDs) to capture core semantic components while reducing dimensionality.Topic Modeling: Developed 10 multi-term topics to provide interpretable features for regression and decision tree models.### 3. Predictive Modeling & EvaluationModels were tested across three categories—Dog Food, Coffee, and Coconut Water—to evaluate performance across different consumer contexts.Product CategoryChampion ModelValidation ROC IndexDog FoodRegression (No Term Weights)0.897 CoffeeRegression (No Term Weights)0.748 Coconut WaterRegression (Entropy Weighting)0.666 ## Key FindingsChampion Performance: The Dog Food regression model achieved a high ROC of 0.897, indicating exceptional reliability in predicting sentiment based on review text.Predictive Drivers: Ingredient transparency is a primary driver of sentiment; topics containing specific nutritional terms were the strongest predictors of positive vs. negative reviews.
