# Amazon-food-review-sentiment-analysis--Text-mining-and-predictive-modelling
Amazon Food Reviews Sentiment analysis: Text Mining & Business Intelligence
This project leverages a massive dataset of over 568,000 Amazon product reviews to transform raw consumer sentiment into actionable business intelligence. By utilizing advanced text mining and predictive modeling, the analysis identifies the specific linguistic drivers that correlate with customer satisfaction and disappointment.
Business Impact This framework allows manufacturers and retailers to move beyond simple star ratings to understand the "why" behind consumer behavior:Product Quality Optimization: Identifies specific ingredient-related drivers—such as "corn," "meal," and "meat"—that directly correlate with sentiment in high-performing categories like Dog Food.Strategic Feature Mapping: Distinguishes between sensory experiences (e.g., "bitter," "smooth," "refreshing") to guide flavor profile development for beverages like Coffee and Coconut Water.Operational Efficiency: Demonstrates how specific text-weighting techniques (like Entropy) can refine model accuracy, allowing for more reliable automated customer feedback loops.Customer Experience (CX) Insights: Provides manufacturers with patterns to proactively address common complaints before they impact brand reputation.
Technical Methodology - SAS tEXT miner was used .The project follows a rigorous data science pipeline to ensure both model performance and semantic interpretability.1. Data Engineering & Pre-processingTarget Engineering: Created a binary sentiment target where scores $\ge 4$ are Positive (1) and $< 4$ are Negative (0).Category Focus: Selected specific products—Dog Food, Coffee, and Coconut Water—to evaluate model performance across diverse consumer contexts.Linguistic Parsing: Implemented tokenization, lemmatization, and custom filtering to remove noise, such as HTML tags and generic terms.2. Feature ExtractionLatent Semantic Analysis: Transformed raw text into 50 Singular Value Decompositions (SVDs) to capture core semantic components while reducing dimensionality.Topic Modeling: Developed 10 multi-term topics to provide interpretable features for the predictive models.3. Predictive Modeling & EvaluationModels were evaluated using a 70/30 stratified train/test split to ensure results were generalizable and not skewed by class imbalance.Product CategoryChampion ModelValidation ROC IndexDog FoodRegression (No Term Weights)0.897CoffeeRegression (No Term Weights)0.748Coconut WaterRegression (Entropy Weighting)0.666Champion Model Insight: The Dog Food regression model achieved the highest reliability with an ROC of 0.897, indicating exceptional precision in predicting sentiment based on review text
Key FindingsIngredient Transparency: For the highest-performing models, ingredient quality (e.g., "first meat") was a primary driver of positive sentiment.Methodological Value: The study proves that while standard regression works well for straightforward categories like Dog Food, complex beverages benefit from specific entropy-based filtering to capture nuanced feedback.
